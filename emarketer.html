Measurement and Budget Go Hand in Hand

With each passing year, more marketers are using social media, and their investments continue to grow. While some are increasing expenditures because they have had positive results, many more are investing in the hope that social media will pay dividends in the future. "It's a Catch-22," said Sarah Buchanan, director of digital strategy at roundhouse, a digital agency, in an interview with eMarketer. "We need to prove the worth of social but at the same time we can't get the resources and the budgets to prove it until we’ve proved that social works," In a February 2011 survey conducted by MarketingSherpa, 47% of companies in the strategic phase of social media development— with a formal process and thorough guidelines for social media marketing—said they were able to measure ROI and would continue to spend money. However, only 17% of companies in the transition stage (with an informal process and a few guidelines for using social media marketing) and 16% in the trial stage of development (with no process or guidelines) agreed that they could measure ROI. Most companies in these phases were spending conservatively in the hopes of eventually producing ROI.
